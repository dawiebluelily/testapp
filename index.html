WHY SELL AT MARKET
VALUE?

Faster Sale:
Homes priced correctly from the start tend to sell quicker, reducing the time your property sits on the market and the risk of it becoming "stale."

Attracts More Buyers:
A well-priced home draws more attention from serious buyers and increases the chances of receiving multiple offers.

Maximizes Sale Price:
Setting the right price can create urgency and competition, which can sometimes drive the final sale price above asking.

Avoids Price Reductions:
Overpricing often leads to necessary price cuts, which can signal to buyers that there is something wrong with the home or make them wait for further reductions.

Appraisal Success:
A correctly priced home is more likely to appraise at value, avoiding financing issues or deal fall-throughs due to a low appraisal.

THE RISKS
• Under-pricing may achieve more views, however it may not lead to a sale.
• The risk of over and under-pricing can delay the transaction.
• Overpricing poses the risk that the banks not approving bonds due to value.
• Under-pricing poses the risk that you sell for less that you need to sell.

THE MANDATORY DISCLOSURE FORM
A seller's disclosure is mandatory if you are selling your property through a property practitioner. The seller is encouraged to disclose all the patent (known) and latent (unknown) defects which exist in the property to any prospective purchaser. This is achieved by completing a document known as the Immovable Property Condition Report which sets out various statements regarding the condition of the property.

FICA DOCUMENTS
The Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA) places stringent obligations on all attorneys and property practitioners to verify certain information and keep records thereof. To ensure compliance with their statutory obligations, both your conveyancing attorney and property practitioner will request certain documents from you.

COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATES
A legal obligation to provide various compliance certificates exists when a property is being transferred. These certificates must be obtained by the seller at his own cost, from a reputable and registered service provider.

These include:
• Electrical compliance certificate (includes solar installation)
• Gas conformity certificate (if applicable)
• Electrical fence certificate (if applicable)

RATES & LEVY CERTIFICATES
The conveyancing attorney is obliged to lodge a rates clearance certificate which must be valid until at least date of registration of the transfer.

The conveyancing attorney will obtain rates clearance figures from the municipality which include an estimated forward projection for a period of 4 months in respect of rates and utilities as well as any arrear amounts.

SUSPENSIVE CONDITIONS
Sale agreements usually contains one or more suspensive conditions. The sale agreement will not be enforceable and binding if these suspensive conditions are not fulfilled completely and timeously.

Any deviation from the terms of a suspensive condition must be addressed in an addendum before its due date.
